Structural System Overview

Structural systems are the main load-bearing components of PEB structures including columns, rafters, and end wall posts. Their design depends on building usage, load conditions, and span requirements.

High Strength

Secondary Structural Members

Secondary framing includes cold-formed steel members such as purlins, girts, and eave struts, designed to support cladding and distribute loads efficiently.

  • Material: Cold-formed steel
  • Yield Strength: 345 MPa (50,000 psi)
  • Standards: ASTM A1011 Grade 50 / ASTM A653 Grade 50

Connection Systems

Reliable structural connections designed for optimal load transfer, fast assembly, and long-term joint integrity.

Overlapping Connection

Utilizes structural overlap to ensure seamless capacity transfer and deflection control in secondary members.

Butt Connection

Direct flushed connections for rigid joining of structural segments, ensuring precise alignment and shear resistance.

Sleeve Connection

Employs robust insert sleeves to maintain continuity across sections, providing superior rotational stiffness.
